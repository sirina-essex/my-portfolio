About Me

With nearly 5 years of experience in both functional and technical roles in IT, I have gained a solid understanding of IT infrastructures and the associated security challenges. This diverse experience has allowed me to navigate both the technical complexities and the functional management of IT projects. As a dynamic professional, I have developed key skills in problem-solving and technological risk analysis.

Currently, I am pursuing an MSc in Cybersecurity at the University of Essex, where I explore a wide range of topics, from vulnerability management to network security, digital forensics, and cybersecurity law. This program enables me to tackle complex themes such as critical infrastructure protection, threat modeling, and incident response. Through hands-on projects and in-depth research, I am building a solid foundation to confidently address critical challenges in cybersecurity, while devising innovative solutions to emerging threats.

I am passionate about analyzing and exploring information security systems, with a particular interest in critical thinking and delving deeply into both theoretical and practical concepts. This approach allows me to fully grasp the complex layers of cybersecurity and to develop creative solutions to increasingly sophisticated security issues.
