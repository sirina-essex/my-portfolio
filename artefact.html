Reflective Activity : Ethics in Computing in the age of Generative AI

This reflective analysis explores the ethical and governance challenges raised by the use of generative AI in computing. It critically examines institutional weaknesses, fragmented ethical norms, and the unrealistic expectations placed on individual professionals, drawing on the work of Correa (2023), Deckard (2023), and others to propose a contextualised model of ethical governance.

Connected Medical Implants: Ethics, Vulnerabilities, and Governance Failures

Critical analysis of cybersecurity flaws in medical technologies, based on a case study of Bluetooth-enabled cardiac implants. The discussion applies the ACM and BCS Codes of Ethics to highlight violations of risk governance, lack of transparency, and the ethical consequences of leaving known vulnerabilities uncorrected.

Manipulating Data, Bending Ethics: The Case of Whizzz Cereal

Critical discussion of statistical responsibility and data integrity in industry-funded research. Based on the case of Abi and the Whizzz cereal study, this analysis examines ethical tensions between neutrality and narrative framing, explores the risk of cherry-picking in data reporting, and applies international codes of ethics to assess accountability, whistleblowing duties, and the societal impact of minimising public health risks.
