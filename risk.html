Security and Risk Management

Learning Outcomes

  • Understanding the definitions and fundamental concepts of risk management

    • Identify and apply key definitions of risk according to normative frameworks such as ISO 31000, ISO 27005, and NIST SP 800-30. Differentiate between qualitative and quantitative approaches in risk evaluation.

  • Threat modeling and vulnerability management

    • Utilize threat modeling techniques such as STRIDE, Attack Trees, and PASTA to identify, categorize, and prioritize threats in complex systems. Analyze specific threat scenarios using frameworks such as MITRE ATT&CK and OWASP to strengthen the security of critical infrastructures.

  • Utilization of security standards in various industry sectors

    • Apply security standards such as GDPR, ISO 27001, and PCI DSS to ensure regulatory compliance and the security of information systems across different sectors. Integrate security standards into risk management models to minimize risks related to data protection and cyberattacks.

  • Application of quantitative techniques for risk management

    • Apply quantitative risk modeling techniques such as Monte Carlo simulations, Bayesâ€™ theorem, and multicriteria methods (AHP, ANP) to quantify uncertainties and prioritize risks in complex environments. Select and justify the appropriate quantitative techniques based on specific risk management scenarios.

  • Development and implementation of business continuity and disaster recovery (BC/DR) plans

    • Design business continuity and disaster recovery plans based on Recovery Time Objective (RTO) and Recovery Point Objective (RPO), in accordance with standards such as ISO 22301. Assess and recommend appropriate DR solutions, such as Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS), to ensure fast and effective recovery after a disaster.

  • Critical analysis of emerging trends in security risk management

    • Identify and analyze future trends in SRM, such as critical system resilience, artificial intelligence (AI), and the Internet of Things (IoT). Evaluate the impact of new technologies and the ethical challenges associated with their integration into critical infrastructures.

  • Resilience and proactive risk management

    • Develop resilience strategies to ensure service continuity in critical environments despite cyberattacks. Integrate AI-based proactive cybersecurity solutions to automate threat detection and response, while addressing ethical and regulatory aspects.

  • Ethical and regulatory approach to security risk management

    • Recognize the ethical, legal, and regulatory issues in using advanced technologies such as AI and IoT to ensure data protection and compliance. Integrate these considerations into risk management strategies to ensure security while respecting users' rights.
