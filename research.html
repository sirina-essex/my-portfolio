Research Methods and Professional Practice

Learning Outcomes

  • Mastering the scientific method and forms of reasoning

    • Differentiate between inductive and deductive reasoning and apply them to academic and professional investigations in computing.

  • Understanding ethical, legal and professional responsibilities

    • Appraise and integrate key ethical principles such as respect, beneficence, justice, and legal accountability in the design and conduct of research involving human participants or sensitive data.

  • Formulating research questions and proposals

    • Transform research ideas into structured questions and craft coherent research proposals grounded in relevant literature and methodological considerations.

  • Critically reviewing academic literature

    • Conduct comprehensive literature reviews to identify existing research gaps, assess methodologies, and synthesise findings aligned with research objectives.

  • Selecting and applying research methods

    • Identify and justify the use of qualitative, quantitative, or mixed methods approaches for primary and secondary data collection in computing contexts.

  • Designing data collection instruments and techniques

    • Develop, test and refine tools such as questionnaires, interview guides, observation protocols, and surveys tailored to research goals.

  • Applying descriptive and inferential statistics

    • Use statistical techniques to summarise, interpret and test hypotheses based on quantitative datasets, including the use of appropriate probability distributions and hypothesis tests.

  • Analysing and presenting qualitative and quantitative data

    • Employ coding strategies for qualitative data and data visualisation techniques to communicate research findings effectively through dashboards, graphs and structured narratives.

  • Ensuring validity, reliability, and generalisability

    • Evaluate and enhance the robustness of research designs by assessing measurement validity, methodological reliability and the generalisability of findings.

  • Demonstrating professional growth through reflective practice

    • Produce an e-Portfolio that documents skills, learning, and professional development throughout the programme using evidence-based reflection aligned with the BCS Code of Conduct.
